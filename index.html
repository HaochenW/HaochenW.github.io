About

I build generative AI and language models for biological sequences to explore DNA/RNA space, design functional regulatory elements, and improve interpretability of genomic foundation models. I hold a B.Sc. in Artificial Intelligence (Xidian University) and a Ph.D. in Control Science & Bioinformatics (Tsinghua University, advisor: Prof. Xiaowo Wang). I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Yale University.

  • Focus: generative models for promoter/enhancer/UTR design; LLMs for RNA interpretation; mechanistic interpretability.
  • Tooling: PyTorch, TensorFlow, Python, Linux, Docker, AWS; MPRA/STARR-seq analysis.

Projects

GAN-based de novo promoter design for E. coli
70.8% functional rate; low similarity to native promoters.
  • Two-stage system: generator explores sequence space; predictor filters high-activity candidates.
  • Wet-lab validation with FACS-based assays.
  • Code
DeepSEED — Flanking sequence engineering
Constitutive + IPTG/Dox-inducible promoters with improved performance.
  • Hybrid approach combining domain motifs (seed) and deep generative modeling (flanks).
  • Validated across prokaryotic and mammalian systems.
  • Code
Orthogonal promoter design with gradient-based optimization
  • Designed orthogonal promoters from scratch with tunable activity.
  • Cross-system applicability (prokaryote/eukaryote).
  • Code
